\ \ \ Inflation Eats 74% of Your Returns Over 20 Years!\ \ \ \

\
\
\ \

Hello there!

Over a 20-year holding period, assuming a 14% nominal return from the Nifty and 6% inflation, inflation eats ~74% of your total returns.

\

Let me say that differently. You think you made 14% a year, but most of that gain is an illusion created by a falling rupee.\

\

Your actual purchasing power grew at barely 8% a year. The other 6%? Vanished into your cost of living.\

\

The irony is, you still pay capital gains tax on the full 14%, including the portion that never made you richer.

\

Indexation once softened this blow. But after recent budget changes, most assets are now taxed on nominal gains.

\

That\'92s why portfolios look rich on screens but feel underwhelming in real life.

\

That gap between what your statement shows and what your wallet feels? That's inflation. And over two decades, it takes \uc0\u8377 74 of every \u8377 100 of return you thought you earned.

So\'85\

    \
  • \ Before you buy:

    Calculate your personal inflation rather than the government\'92s CPI. If you spend on private schools or healthcare, use 8-9%.\

    Check how the asset is taxed.

    \uc0\u55357 \u56393 Equities and mutual funds are taxed at 12.5% with no indexation.
    \u55357 \u56393 Real estate gets indexation only if bought before July 2024.
    \u55357 \u56393 SGBs are tax-free only if you\'92re the original subscriber holding till maturity.\

    • \
  • \ Every year:

    Divide your current corpus by 1.06 for each year you\'92ve held it.\

    \ \ Example: 5 years = divide by 1.06 five times. If it\'92s flat or down, you\'92re not growing.\ \

    • \
  • \ Before you sell:

    \ Celebrate only returns above 6% per year. A 2x in 12 years is a zero real return.\ \
