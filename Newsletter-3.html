Why Everything Looks Investable Right Now

Most stocks are repricing for a reason. The few that aren't, become structural outliers.

During a correction, the optical discount widens as trailing multiples compress and forward P/E moves back toward familiar ranges.

That’s when screens start flagging a lot more “value.”

What you’re really seeing:

  • Proximity to 52-week troughs
  • 30-40% drawdowns across sectors
  • Multiples reverting to historical bands

It's useful, but also incomplete. Price is a lagging output; it flattens everything into a single number.

Two businesses can land at the same multiple. One is still improving underneath, and the other is just managing appearances. That’s what creates much confusion.

But you can see a clearer pattern if you simply move your focus towards how management is actually deploying capital:

  • Promoters buying more shares from the market
  • Companies holding back from expanding into unexplored areas
  • Cash flows staying strong relative to reported profits
  • Management focusing on margins and execution over future opportunities

None of this creates clean entry signals. But it reveals how a business behaves when the cost of being wrong is high.

So, if your current filter is struggling to separate value traps from actual compounders this market, this video should give you a clearer lens.
