\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ Investing in the Time of Global Unrest\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \

\
\
\ \

Strange how \'93being careful\'94 starts feeling like \'93being right\'94 around times like these.\

What investors (or anybody, as a matter of fact,) crave for at the moment is certainty. Where capital feels safer sitting in cash, or gold, or anything that doesn\'92t move too much.

\

That pull has a name: pseudocertainty. When the world feels unstable, a 90% safe option starts feeling like 100%.

\

But this way, you stop questioning what that \'93safety\'94 actually costs you over the next decade.

\ \

At the same time, your imagination gets louder than your math.

\

You read enough about conflict, supply shocks, tail risks, and as a consequence, unlikely events stop feeling unlikely.

\

This is probability neglect. The mind stops asking how likely, and starts acting as if the worst case is simply what\'92s next. Portfolios shift to survive extremes rather than partaking in reality.

\

And when it comes to what you already own, it all starts passing a lower bar.

Holding feels justified without much scrutiny. A stock would look questionable if it were someone else\'92s idea. In your portfolio, it feels like conviction.

\

None of these decisions will feel wrong when you make them. And that\'92s the problem.

\

So instead of trying to predict geopolitics, watch for something subtler; the moment your thinking starts bending around it.

From Ticker: The $100 Oil Impact

\

Crude crossing $100 is starting to show up across markets. For India, with heavy import dependence, the impact is real but not uniform.

\

The pressure builds in oil-linked sectors, like paints, aviation, tyres, where input costs move almost directly with crude.

At the same time, a weaker Rupee quietly supports export-heavy spaces like IT and pharma.

\

What to do? Don't panic sell:\

Instead, check if your portfolio can survive inflation. Log into Ticker and check your stocks for:\

    \
  • Pricing Power: Look at their Operating Margins over the last 5 years. If they maintained margins during past oil spikes, they are resilient.
    • \
  • Low Debt: Run a check for Debt-to-Equity < 0.5. High oil means high inflation, which keeps interest rates high. Low-debt companies survive this best.
    • \
  • Core Efficiency: Use the DuPont Analysis to see exactly how they are generating returns and if their core operations are still healthy.
